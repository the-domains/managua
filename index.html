There Is Nowhere Else Quite Like Managua

1989 – “Managua is scattered—as if some god had tossed a handful of the city’s fine dust to the wind or heaved out a splattering of rainy-season mud. But two other primeval forces have been at work on the city: earthquakes and capitalism. Anastasio Somoza called the 1972 earthquake a “revolution of possibilities” and for him and his associates it was. They bought up large tracts on the city’s fringes and had their companies rebuild housing on their land. The result is a third-world Los Angeles, laid out for the motorcar with suburbs spread about shopping malls. Yet in Managua there are few cars and the malls are covered market stalls. The crammed bus is the main form of transport.” read more
