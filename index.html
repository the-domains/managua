Managua is a mess. Flattened by the 1972 earthquake; and impoverished by civil war and imperial aggression; Managua has never recovered. Its downtown will never be rebuilt. It was built on an earthquake fault next to Lake Managua. Only a few edifices remain, like the Ruben Dario theater, and the Palacio Nacional. The Cathedral stands in ruins, with an old movie theater just behind it. The Telcor building is there, up from the Plaza de la Revolución, still being used as the central post office and telephone exchange.
