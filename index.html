Managua, the capital city of Nicaragua, has been described as “among the ugliest capital cities in the [Western] hemisphere”. Tourists are advised to spend as little time as possible in the metropolis, and some tour operators even skip it altogether, preferring to ferry visitors directly from the city’s international airport to the luxury self-contained seaside resort of Montelimar or the historic colonial cities of León and Granada.
